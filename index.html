MASO

The Work

MASO creates architectural audio objects that exist between art, furniture, and loudspeaker. Each piece is conceived as a permanent element of a space rather than a removable device.

Materials, proportion, and spatial presence are treated with the same seriousness as acoustic performance. The result is sound that is integrated, calm, and precise — never visually dominant, yet deeply present.

Every work is developed as a limited piece, fabricated with architectural processes and intended to age with the building it inhabits.

The Author

MASO was founded by designer Guy Traviss as an exploration of how sound objects can operate within architectural space without behaving like conventional consumer electronics.

The practice draws from modernist architecture, gallery display systems, and industrial material language to produce objects that are both functional and spatially intentional.

The aim is not decoration, but integration — sound as part of the built environment.
