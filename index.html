The Work

MASO explores sound as material — not as background, but as structure. Each work seeks permanence through form, allowing sound to inhabit space with the same authority as architecture or sculpture.

Rather than devices, the objects operate as presences: quiet, deliberate, and designed to endure beyond cycles of technology or fashion.

The intention is not spectacle, but integration — sound embedded into lived environments with restraint and clarity.

The Author

Guy Traviss is a designer working at the intersection of sound, architecture, and object design. His work focuses on permanence, reduction, and the cultural role of technology in domestic space.

Drawing from architectural thinking as much as audio engineering, MASO represents an attempt to produce objects that remain relevant over decades rather than years.
