The Work

MASO is an exploration of sound as a permanent architectural presence. Rather than conventional loudspeakers, each work functions as a spatial object — an artefact designed to exist within the language of architecture, not consumer electronics.

Materials, proportions and structural expression are treated as carefully as acoustic performance, allowing the work to operate simultaneously as sound system and art object.

The Author

Guy Traviss is a designer working at the intersection of architecture, sound and material culture. His work explores how technology can be integrated into living environments without visual intrusion.

MASO represents a shift from products to artefacts — objects intended to endure both physically and culturally.
